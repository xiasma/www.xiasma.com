Services

What Xiasma builds.

Xiasma designs and develops specialist web applications for organisations with complex workflows, unusual data, or domain-specific requirements. We are particularly interested in science, health, research, public-sector and operational software.

Service areas

Where Xiasma is most useful.

Specialist SaaS products

Design and development of focused web products for niche workflows, including UX, architecture and long-term technical stewardship.

Scientific and health software

Applications involving biological, clinical, epidemiological, research or laboratory data, where domain accuracy and data quality matter.

Workflow and case-management systems

Tools for replacing spreadsheets, email chains and manual operational processes with structured, auditable, maintainable software.

Dashboards and reporting tools

Interfaces for understanding structured data, tracking status, and generating outputs for teams, partners or stakeholders.

Integration and data engineering

APIs, import and export tools, data cleaning, migration, and connections between specialist or legacy systems.

Long-term product support

Maintenance, iteration, hosting support and technical stewardship for software that needs to keep working for years.

How we work

Carefully and predictably.

Most engagements begin with a short, scoped piece of work — usually a discovery and specification phase — so both sides understand the workflow and the build before committing further. From there we move into iterative delivery against an agreed roadmap.

Engagements are sized to fit the work. We are comfortable with regulated environments, scientific and clinical data, legacy integrations and the kinds of constraints that surround them.

  • Short discovery first — model the workflow before scoping the build.
  • Working software early — visible progress in weeks, not quarters.
  • Honest scopes — we'd rather decline than over-promise.
  • Long-term stewardship — Xiasma can stay with a product for years.
  • UK-based delivery — working with UK and international teams.
